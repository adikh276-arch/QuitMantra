What is the DSM-5?

The DSM-5 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition) is the authoritative guide published by the American Psychiatric Association for diagnosing mental disorders. First released in 2013, it was updated as the DSM-5-TR in 2022.

For substance-related conditions, the DSM-5 replaced the older distinction between "substance abuse" and "substance dependence" with a single diagnosis: Substance Use Disorder. Severity is measured on a continuum, making it more clinically useful than earlier editions.

The 11 DSM-5 criteria

Each question in this quiz maps to one of these diagnostic criteria.

Impaired Control
  • 1 Using in larger amounts or longer than intended
  • 2 Wanting to cut down but being unable to
  • 3 Excessive time spent obtaining, using, or recovering
  • 4 Cravings or strong urges to use
Social Impairment
  • 5 Failure to fulfill obligations at work, school, or home
  • 6 Continued use despite relationship problems
  • 7 Giving up or reducing important activities
Risky Use
  • 8 Recurrent use in physically hazardous situations
  • 9 Continued use despite physical or psychological harm
Pharmacological Indicators
  • 10 Tolerance: needing more for the same effect
  • 11 Withdrawal symptoms when stopping or reducing use

Frequently asked questions

The DSM-5 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition) is the standard classification of mental disorders published by the American Psychiatric Association. It is used by clinicians, researchers, and insurance companies worldwide to diagnose and classify mental health conditions, including Substance Use Disorders.
The DSM-5 defines Substance Use Disorder (SUD) as a cluster of cognitive, behavioral, and physiological symptoms indicating that an individual continues using a substance despite significant substance-related problems. The DSM-5 identifies 11 criteria grouped into four categories: impaired control, social impairment, risky use, and pharmacological indicators (tolerance and withdrawal).
The DSM-5 uses a severity spectrum based on how many of the 11 criteria are met within a 12-month period. Meeting 2 to 3 criteria indicates a mild Substance Use Disorder, 4 to 5 criteria indicates moderate severity, and 6 or more criteria indicates a severe Substance Use Disorder.
